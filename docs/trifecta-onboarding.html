Tip

03
Install Real Estate Trifecta.
Under the new real-estate-trifecta marketplace, find the Real Estate Trifecta card and click the + to install it.
Tip

04
Just start using it. There's no setup command.
Each skill runs a short interview the very first time you call on it (four or five questions), saves your answers, and never asks again. So the setup is the first use. Open a new chat and describe the moment you're walking into, like the example below.
I'm meeting a buyer tomorrow at 10. Here's what she said in her inquiry...
Tip
The same works for the other two: "I have a listing appointment Thursday, here are the comps" wakes up the Listing Presentation, and "Offer accepted on 42 Maple! Buyers, closing March 15" wakes up Closing Day.

Good to know
Where your deliverables go

All three skills work fully in chat with nothing connected. With your tools connected, they do more: every guide, deck, and leave-behind saves to a clearly named Google Drive folder per client or listing. Calendar holds your consultations, contract deadlines, and the 30-day and one-year touches. Gmail gets your follow-ups as ready-to-send drafts (they never send). And Claude in Chrome can read the lead, the comps, or the accepted offer straight off your CRM or MLS screen, so you're not re-typing.

If you connected your tools on Team day, you're already done. Otherwise: Customize > Connectors > +, pick a tool, sign in once.

Getting updates

When the Trifecta is improved, you don't reinstall anything.

In Customize > Plugins, click the ··· next to the real-estate-trifecta marketplace and choose Update. Claude pulls the latest version. Your interview answers and personalization stay exactly as you set them up.

FAQ

Do I need the Team for this to work?+
No. The Trifecta stands on its own. But if you hired the Team, they hand off to each other: Lia markets the listing you win, Treena runs the transaction, Olivia drills the objections before your appointment, and Sloane keeps the relationship warm after closing day.
Is there a /set-me-up for the Trifecta?+
No, and that's on purpose. Each skill asks you four or five questions the first time you use it, saves your answers, and never asks again. Just describe the meeting or the deal you're walking into and it takes it from there.
How is this different from Lia and Treena?+
The Trifecta is for the three make-or-break moments: meeting a buyer, winning a listing, and getting a deal to the keys. Lia markets a listing you've already won. Treena runs the week-to-week transaction communication in the middle. Closing Day hands the middle stretch to Treena if she's hired, and takes back over at the closing itself.
Where do the guides and presentations save?+
With Google Drive connected, everything saves automatically to a clearly named folder per client or listing, and you get the link. Without Drive, you get the files for download in chat and can put them wherever you like.
I'm in Canada. Does Closing Day get my process right?+
Yes. Closing Day builds the roadmap for your country and region: subjects and completion day in Canada, contingencies and settlement in the US, with the right words for deposits, lawyers, notaries, title, and escrow. It also adapts to the property type, so a condo, a new build, and a detached resale each get their own path.
A skill didn't wake up when I asked. What do I do?+
Name the moment more directly: "help me prep for a buyer consultation," "build my listing presentation," or "offer accepted, build the road to closing." If it still doesn't fire, confirm the plugin is installed (Customize > Plugins), start a brand new chat, or close and reopen the desktop app.
Will it make up prices or comps?+
No. The pricing story only uses the real comparable sales you provide, and it asks for them if you haven't shared any. Cost figures in the Home Buyer Guide are always labeled as illustrative planning ranges, never a quote. And nothing gets sent, posted, or shared without you saying go.
